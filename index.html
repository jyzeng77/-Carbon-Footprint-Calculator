Using the data you provided, we estimated how much carbon emissions you emit monthly, as shown above. You may have noticed the "e" in CO2e. CO2e stands for the "carbon dioxide equivalent," which tells you how much CO2 is needed to be released in order to have the same amount of environmental impact. Since there are many different greenhouse gases other than carbon dioxide, scientists use the carbon dioxide equivalent to compare the effects of these gases in a standardized way.
